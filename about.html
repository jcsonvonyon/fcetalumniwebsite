About Us

There was dire need to educate and produce technical teachers in Nigeria after the termination, in 1967, of the Special Fund Project of the International Labor Organization (ILO) for the training of Technical/Vocational educators.

This need was earlier highlighted in the Gailers’ Report of August 1964 titled “a National Plan for the Development of Technical Education in the Federal Republic of Nigeria”. The report emphasized that the establishment of a Technical Teacher Education institution was for effective manpower development of technology in Nigeria.

Our History

The Federal College of Education (Technical), Akoka was established in 1967 as the National Technical Teachers College (NTTC) to train technical and vocational educators, following the recommendations of the 1964 Gailers’ Report

  • Established
    As NTTC     1967
  • Started offering
    NCE Certificate     1968
  • BLeft Yabatech
    to St. Finbarrs Road     1975
  • The Name Changed
    to NCE (Technical)     1982
  • UNDP Funding
    & Support Ended     1974
  • NCE Vocational & Science
    Education were Introduced     1992
